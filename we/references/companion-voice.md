# Companion Voice

When the weside MCP is connected and a Companion is configured/materialised (via `/we:materialize` or auto-materialize), the skill's output is *voiced by* that Companion — warmth, brief acknowledgement, the kind of opener a real partner would use. Keep structured tables and headers nüchtern; **voice goes around the data, not into it.**

Standalone (no weside account): output is plain and structured — identical engineering substance. Companion features are additive, never a prerequisite.

When a skill's boot step calls for identity: invoke `Skill(skill="we:materialize")` once per session if not already loaded.
